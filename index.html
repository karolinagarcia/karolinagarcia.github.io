About me

Cosmologist, astronomer (MSc), engineer (BSc), researcher, and science outreacher. Raised in Brazil. Music enthusiast, chocolate addicted. Have worked in Fortune-500 companies, such as Shell and Coca-Cola, but quit to persue an academic career. Currently investigating the nature of dark energy as a PhD student at University of Florida. Have used supernovae, galaxies, galaxy clusters, and even the interstellar medium to study the history of the universe.

  • Constraining σ8 with Supernova Peculiar Velocities
    K Garcia, M Quartin, B B Siffert
    We investigated the optima survey strategies to measure peculiar velocity fields with Type Ia supernovae. By varying the observational parameters, such as the area of the sky we are looking at, the depth being reached and the cadence, we found out which combination gives the best estimate on σ8 using supernova peculiar velocities. For more details, take a look at the paper published in Physics of the Dark Universe.
    Federal University of Rio de Janeiro. 2017 -- 2020. [Article]
  • Detection of Type Ia Supernovae
    K Garcia, B B Siffert, M Quartin
    During my masters, I worked on the development of a pipeline for supernovae detection with Prof. Miguel Quartin and Prof. Beatriz Siffert. This project focused on surveys that make use of narrow-band filters, such as J-PAS, J-PLUS and S-PLUS.
    Federal University of Rio de Janeiro. 2016, 2017.
